## The problem
Reddit is where your buyers say what they actually want — which tool to switch to, what's broken,
who to hire. But it's a haystack, and bolting a bot onto it gets you banned. You need the signal,
not another spam machine.

## How GTMCat works
**1. Capture — through your own session.**
The GTMCat Capture extension reads the public Reddit threads your own logged-in session can already
see. No Reddit API, no scraping service, no credentials stored. If your session can't see it,
GTMCat doesn't fetch it.

**2. Score — by buyer intent.**
Every captured thread is scored 0–100 for buyer intent against your product context, and tagged
(active purchase, switching, pain point, recommendation ask, budget signal, competitor frustration).
The noise drops away.

**3. Engage — by hand, from your account.**
GTMCat reads each subreddit's rules for you, drafts a humanized reply that fits the room, and hands
it to you. **You** post it, from **your** account. No votes, no sock-puppets, no autopilot — ever.

## The line we don't cross
GTMCat is read-only capture and human-in-the-loop engagement. It never posts, comments, votes, or
automates anything on Reddit. Capture profiles are throwaway read-only logins; your posting identity
is a username only. That's not a limitation — it's the only way to do this without burning your
brand. [Read our approach →](./privacy.md)
